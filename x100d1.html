The Rise of Hackers

The term "hacker" originally referred to someone who made furniture with an axe. However, as technology evolved, so did the meaning of the word. In the 1960s, the first computer hackers emerged, mainly in university settings, where they sought to push the boundaries of technologies. By the 1980s, hacking had entered the public consciousness, fueled by media coverage of high-profile hacks and the emergence of computer networks. The hacker subculture began to flourish, giving rise to a diverse community that included how ethical hackers, who sought to improve security, and malicious actors, who exploited vulnerabilities for personal gain.
